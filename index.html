    Fuel to Perform

    Eat Like a Racer

    The goal is fueling and growing — eat ENOUGH, not less

    Build the plate: about half fruit & veg, a quarter quality carbs (rice, pasta, potatoes, oats, bread), a quarter protein (chicken, eggs, fish, beans, yogurt), plus some healthy fat.

    Carbs are your fuel — don't skip them, especially before and after riding. They're what power your race runs.

    Protein at every meal helps you recover from training and grow stronger.

    Eat before AND after training — never ride hard on empty, and refuel within about 45 minutes of finishing.

    Hydrate all day, with extra on Bolton and race days when you're sweating for hours.

    Eat enough. Hard training on a growing body needs plenty of food — being under-fueled makes you slower and raises injury risk. Hunger is a signal to eat, not ignore.

    Staples to keep stocked
    OatsEggsBread / bagels Rice & pastaPotatoesChicken / turkey Canned beansGreek yogurtMilk Peanut butterBananas & fruitFresh + frozen veg CheeseTrail mixGranola barsHoney

    General guidance to build healthy habits. As a growing athlete, if you want a plan tailored to you, a registered sports dietitian or your doctor is the best person to help — and whatever you do, keep eating enough to support training and growth.

    How this works — process vs. outcome goals

    Outcome goals are the result you want — a podium, a top-10. Motivating, but not fully in your control, so hold them loosely, especially on race day.

    Performance goals are measurable standards against yourself — a clean run, beating your own time. More in your control than an outcome.

    Process goals are the actions and cues you control on every ride — look through the exit, heavy feet, run your pre-run routine. These are your daily focus; nail the process and the outcomes tend to follow.

    Set a few in each, then review at the end of the week and carry what's unfinished forward. Framework courtesy of your team's mental-skills coaching.
